Language

The language used must read the same across all documents. This helps readers understand the text and also makes translation and localization easier.

• US English is to be used in all documentation unless specified otherwise by the client.
• Use the 2nd person imperative – see through the reader’s eyes and give direct instructions.

Yes: Click the item that you wish to select.

No:  The user should click the item they need.

• Use present tense, not future:

Yes: The dialog opens…

No:  The dialog will open…

• Use active voice

Not passive, unless avoiding a worse sentence, such as when the subject is unknown or not the focus of the sentence; or in error messages, when the user is the subject and might feel blamed for the error if the active voice were used.

Yes: The system delivers email quickly (Active - the system acts)

No:  Email is delivered quickly (Passive - email is acted on)

Avoid these common passive phrases:

No:  There can be cases...

No:  It can be said...

No:  It should be understood that...

No:  There is...

• Avoid first person (I/we) except in some marketing, legally oriented sections of books, or in some troubleshooting sections.

Use "doc-department recommends" or "we suggest" to encourage the user to take some action. (More personal than the passive "it’s recommended")

• State information positively whenever possible

Using 'not' can make a sentence harder to read and understand:

Yes: Save your work, and then turn off your computer.

No:  It’s not impossible to lose all your work, so back it up to be safe.

Sometimes you may want to use a negative construction for emphasis, as in a caution or warning, for example:

Yes: Do not turn off your computer without saving your work.

• Avoid contractions:

Yes: You cannot move a book from one shelf to another.

No:  You can't move a book from one shelf to another.

• Ending a sentence with a preposition is acceptable to prevent an awkward construction, but avoid stacking prepositional phrases on top of one another.

Yes: Type the text you want to search for.

No:  Type the text for which you want to search. (clumsy)

• Use neutral forms of words rather than masculine forms.

Yes: workforce, sales representative

No:   manpower, salesman

• Avoid the use of the generic pronouns.

No:  She, he, her, and his

Yes: "They" - can be used in singular form
