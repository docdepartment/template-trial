L

labeled | labelled

Use labeled (US English) as standard.

The UK English version is labelled.

left side

Use left side, as two words, not left-hand side.

less than | fewer | under

Use less to refer to a mass amount, value, or degree.

Use fewer to refer to a countable number of items.

Do not use under to refer to a quantity or number.

No:  10 items or less, fewer waste was produced this year

Yes: 10 items or fewer, less waste was produced this year

let, lets

Avoid let or lets in the sense of the software permitting the user to do something. Use you can instead.

list box

Two words. List box is a generic term for any type of dialog option containing a list of items that the user can select.

In text and procedures, refer to a list box by its label and the word list, not list box. Use drop-down when appropriate.

log on (v), log-on (adj)

log on (v), log-on (adj)

Hyphenated as an adjective, two words as a verb.

Yes: Type your log-on password… and then log on.

Do not use logon, log in, log-in, or other variations.

► See "Banned words"
log off (v), log-off (adj)

Two words as a verb, hyphenated as an adjective.

Do not use logout, log out, log-out, or other variations.

► See "Banned words"
look up

Use look up in the sense to search for, as in 'look up a record'.

Do not use to replace see in cross-references.

lowercase

One word.
