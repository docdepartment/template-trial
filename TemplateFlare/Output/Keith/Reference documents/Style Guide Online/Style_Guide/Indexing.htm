Indexing

Indexes are a series of ordered terms that help readers find the information they require. Not all guides have an index, but when they do they should follow these guidelines:

• Index entries should be short (no more than five words).
• Add index entries to the start of a topic.
• All topics must have at least one index entry.
• Avoid the same index word in multiple locations. This creates too many cross-references which leads to poor information retrieval.
• Index entries must refer to the subject matter.
• When creating index entries consider the sort of words and phrases you would expect a reader to use to find the subject matter covered in the current topic. Consider synonyms, inverted phrases and sentence fragments.
• Use lower case entries unless you are referring to a specific proper noun, product name, or element.
