Safety notices

Safety notices are used to highlight important and potentially hazardous information.

The following safety notices comply to the ANSI standard. There are four main notice types:

Warning and caution notices must be used before any procedures or descriptive text that they are referring to.

Notice and tip notices must be at the relevant point in a procedure or descriptive text.

All notice types should be concise and focused on one point of information.

All notice types should be kept to a minimum within topics.

Do not use graphics in a notice apart from the standard safety notice graphics included by default.

Use the following structure for safety notices:

The flag or signal word, accompanied by a standardised graphic.

Risk: what can happen.

Avoidance: how to prevent the risk and damage.

Consequences: what happens if the safety notice is ignored.

Remedy: how to correct a hazard if this warning has been ignored.
