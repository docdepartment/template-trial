Callouts

A callout is a reference line and notation that highlights particular points of interest in a graphic.

Use numbers or letters and a legend.

Avoid explanatory callout text on graphics.

This causes complications when translating content.

Callouts should be arranged on the right side of an image, where possible.

Callouts should be straight lines from the area being highlighted to the number, unless there is not enough space to do this. In this case minimize any corners in callout lines.

There should only ever be 5±2 callouts on a graphic.
